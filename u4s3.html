4.3 Writing Equations of Lines

On August 5, 2010, a gold-and-copper mine collapsed in Chile, trapping 33 miners underground. Rescuers used a drilling rig to reach the miners under the earth’s surface. An escape capsule carried each miner up the shaft at a constant rate as shown in the graph.

  1. At what underground depth were the miners trapped?
  2. How long has a miner been ascending in the escape capsule when he reaches a depth of 300 feet?
  3. How long did it take to bring each miner to the surface?
  4. At what average rate did the escape capsule carry each miner up the shaft?
  5. Write an expression for the depth of the miner during his rescue with respect to time ascending.
  1. Use the graph “People With Health Insurance,” which estimates the U.S. population with health insurance in the given years, to answer the following questions.
    1. How many people had health insurance in 1990?
    2. How many people had health insurance in 1991?
    3. At what rate is the number of people with health insurance increasing each year?
    4. According to the graph, how many people would you expect to have had health insurance in 1988?
    5. According to the graph, how many people would you expect to have had health insurance in 2014?
    6. The actual number of people in the U.S. with health insurance in 2014 was about 276 million. Why do you think this differs from your estimate in question (e)?
  2. Use the graph “Josh’s Car Trip” to answer the following questions.
    1. Is Josh traveling away from home or towards his home during his trip?
    2. How many miles long is Josh’s trip?
    3. How long does Josh’s trip take?

    5. At this rate, how long would it take Josh to complete a 3300 miles trek across the United States? If he could only drive 8 hours a day, how many days would it take him?
  3. Use the graph “Demand Curve” to answer the following questions.
    1. When the cost of the product is $100, how many units are sold?
    2. When the cost of the product is $200, how many units are sold?
    3. When the cost of the product is $300, how many units are sold?
    4. Is demand increasing or decreasing with respect to cost?
    5. What is the rate of change for units sold for every dollar increase?
